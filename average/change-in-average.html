Change in Average - TCS NQT Practice

Q1: Average of 10 numbers is 20. A new number is added to the group, and the average becomes 22. Find the number added.
Original sum = \(10 \times 20 = 200\)
Let the number added = \(x\)
New average: \((200 + x)/11 = 22\)
Solve: \(200 + x = 242 \Rightarrow x = 42\)
Answer: 42
Q2: Average of 8 numbers is 15. One number is removed from the group, and the new average becomes 14. Find the number removed.
Original sum = \(8 \times 15 = 120\)
Let removed number = \(x\)
New sum / 7 = 14 → \(120 - x = 98 \Rightarrow x = 22\)
Answer: 22
Q3: Average of 12 numbers is 30. Three new numbers are added, and the new average becomes 32. Find the sum of the three numbers added.
Original sum = \(12 \times 30 = 360\)
Let sum of three numbers added = \(S\)
New average: \((360 + S)/15 = 32\) → \(360 + S = 480 \Rightarrow S = 120\)
Answer: 120
Q4: Average of 5 numbers is 40. One number is removed, and the average decreases by 5. Find the number removed.
Original sum = \(5 \times 40 = 200\)
Let removed number = \(x\)
New average = 35 → \((200 - x)/4 = 35 \Rightarrow 200 - x = 140 \Rightarrow x = 60\)
Answer: 60
Q5: Average of 6 numbers is 50. Two numbers are added, and the average becomes 52. Find the sum of the two numbers added.
Original sum = \(6 \times 50 = 300\)
Let sum of two numbers = \(S\)
New average: \((300 + S)/8 = 52 \Rightarrow 300 + S = 416 \Rightarrow S = 116\)
Answer: 116
Q6: Average of 10 numbers is 25. Two numbers are removed, and the new average becomes 23. Find the sum of the numbers removed.
Original sum = \(10 \times 25 = 250\)
Let sum of removed numbers = \(S\)
New average: \((250 - S)/8 = 23 \Rightarrow 250 - S = 184 \Rightarrow S = 66\)
Answer: 66

\(236 - x = 210 \Rightarrow x = 26\)
